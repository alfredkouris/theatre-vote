A Note from the Chefs

Tonight, we will attempt to bake a cake live on stage.

Not a metaphorical cake. Not a symbolic cake. A real cake. With ingredients and measurements and timing and consequences. A cake that can collapse. A cake that can burn. A cake that, like theatre itself, exists only for us in this room tonight.

When we first began making this show, we talked about the liveness of theatre. Its immediacy. Its danger. We wanted to find something that had very real consequences and could very easily go wrong.

And then we thought about cake.

Cakes are inherently optimistic. Nobody bakes a cake for a funeral. Cakes arrive at birthdays, weddings, office farewells, apologies, reconciliations. They are edible acts of hope.

This show was built the same way many cakes are. Messily. Experimentally. Sometimes with too much heat. Sometimes not enough. But somehow, through sheer panic, determination, and an alarming amount of vanilla extract, it became something we are deeply proud to share with you.

Thank you for being here. Thank you for choosing live theatre over convenience, over streaming, over staying home.

Bon appétit,
Alfred Kouris & Harry Stacey xx
